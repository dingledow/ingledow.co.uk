Revel lets people share the food they've made and challenge their friends for the best lunch. Achievements and real world rewards give people the incentive to make their own lunch.

Design can lead people to increase their consumption, often through the desirability of products and convenience. The aim of the brief was to use design to persuade people to consume less, reduce waste and to be more efficient with the planet’s resources.

I worked as part of a group to create Revel – an app that allows people to share photos of food that they have made. It goes beyond the 'food porn' trend by allowing people to challenge their friends for the title of best lunch, using a feature called 'Lunch Off'.

These challenges allow people to gain achievements and real world rewards for participating local and environmentally friendly food outlets.

Users can explore popular offers, lunches and Lunch Off challenges in their local area.

Visual Intent

"To create an identity that has a personable approach conveyed through a clean and minimal interface that communicates effectively to a student and young professional audience."
